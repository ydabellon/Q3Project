Tips on how to prevent fire accidents
1. Turn off unused electronics When you leave your room make sure you turn off every electronic device before leaving. It's optional to not unplug electronic devices if you are just going out to get something. Same goes before your leaving your house, make sure everything is fully off and plugged off, because if you don't, possible chance your house will be on fire
2. Use a fire extinguisher If there is fire onsight, find a fire extinguisher as soon as possible. If you don't know how to use a fire extinguisher, you can look at the back where the instructions are. Remember the code PASS.
3. Call the fire department If incase your house is on massive fire and you don't have a fire extinguisher best thing to do is call the fire as soon as possble. However, if you're the one who noticed the fire first, request everyone inside to leave and ask someone to call the fire department plus ambulance.
4. Don't mix water with electronics This is already commonly known but a just a reminder to PLEASE don't mix water with electronics, it's dangerous. If you plugged in a wet cord to an outlet small smoke will come out and can be smelled be easily. What you need to do is to plug it off immediately (after drying your hands) and call an adult to check the situation. To add, not only smoke will happen but possible chance you might get electrucuted.
5. Be careful around fire Last but not the least is to always be careful around fire; when you're cooking, camping, and near with a candle. For cooking, be careful leaving the stove/oven on because if you leave it on for too long it will cause a fire. For camping, just be careful with the wood and fire because if you move the wood (that has fire in it) possible chance you might burn the campsite. Lastly, for candles, be careful when you see a candle that is lit up already because if you knock it over, you just caused fire.
